Instagram & Social Media

Instagram plays a major role in shaping teens' perceptions of themselves. Filters, likes, and comments contribute to unrealistic beauty standards, often causing self-esteem issues. More and more research links Instagram’s features to negative self-image among adolescents. For example:

- Body image concerns: Filters and photo-editing tools promote impossible beauty ideals.

- Cyberbullying: Approximately 20% of teens report fearing online harassment, which has long-term effects on self-esteem.

- Social comparison: Viewing curated and edited content can lead to feelings of imperfection and dissatisfaction with real-life experiences and looks.

Instagram has introduced tools to combat these challenges, such as comment filtering, blocking options, and private account settings. Teens are encouraged to use these features to create safer online environments and limit exposure to toxic content.
